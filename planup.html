Context

During my first year of my Master, I did an Erasmus in the Netherlands. I went to Windesheim in Zwolle and attend a class named Concept and Creation. The goal was to create and develop a project in a multidisciplinary team. We had to design, develop and think of how to market it. During what they called the Dragon's Den we had to defend our concept in front of professionals.

Our idea

Planup is a mobile app to help students in their homework planning. The user just has to write the task they need to do, select the tags related to this task, the due date, and the importance, and Planup will automatically organize the planning. The user can choose the time slot during which they want to work. Want to find the best place to work? Planup

Planning of the project

To plan our work we did multiple documents such as a plan of approach and a feasibility report. The goal of these was to be sure that the project was feasible by checking if our target would be interested, if it can be done in time or if it does already exist. In the plan of approach, we explained how we would work as a team. We did a Risk matrix to understand the possible risks in the process of the project, and a SWOT analysis to see the strengths and weaknesses. Then, we did a stakeholder map to understand the different actors that could impact the project.

Functionalities

Planup have 4 main functionalities:
- The planning (to see all the tasks you have to do)
- Task (to create or edit your task, chose or create tags, and set up the priority)
- Map (to find the best place to work depending on the task, need inspiration? Go to a park! Need calm? Go to this library!)
- The settings (to set your time slot or import your school roster)

User test and interview

To know better our users, I started by interviewing different Dutch and international students. I asked them about how they plan they work if they do, where they are working, when, and the different hobbies they might have.
I also did some user tests on my first prototype to check if the concept was adapted to our users. It resulted in a few changes for the next version of the wireframe.

Graphical Identity

We created a simple and clear identity based on 4 colors (red, blue, green, and yellow) We are using them for the 4 types of places that are silent, comfortable, with food, and inspirational place. Blue is the main color because it transmits calm and serenity.

How to market it?

For the marketing, we thought of starting by presenting Planup to the Windesheim students. The communication would also be done through social media thanks to an account that would give organization advice for students. Our map feature is presenting you with the best places to work. By having a partnership with a cafe or library we can advertise for each other.

Conclusion

It was the first time that I was working with a multidisciplinary and multicultural team. I learned a lot about team management, documentation, and marketing. I was able to share my knowledge about design, I had to explain my job and the use of all the steps I was doing.
