Context

Our first Workshop, a project in only 5 days. This Quick and Dirty Workshop was obviously stressful and interesting.

We worked with the Design Sprint method to improve a web site we choose. Our group was composed of 5 members and we quickly found how to work effectively together. Tom was the one charged to motivate the group, Paco did all the illustrations, Isaac was in charge of connecting all the pages, Enora wrote the texts and I had to propose ideas of compositions.

The site

Enora proposed us the website because she knew the association. Suzie Handicap Animal is an association that welcomed handicapped animals. Stéphanie feed them, cure them and, for some, try to find them a new family.

We went through the site and found a lot of colours and communication issues. The home page was okay, but after it, the rest was not really pretty. Nevertheless, the teachers told us to focus our energy on the real objective of the website.

Finding our Angle

After discussing with the association, we determined that the main problem to solve was a lot of people wanted to adopt without knowing the consequences of having an animal, nor the expenses related to. So we thought about different way to raise awareness about these animals and show the different solutions to help them.

Each one of us had to develop an idea by drawing a Usage scenario. The ideas we developed were: an advertising in a newspaper, an infographic to explain how to adopt an animal and a handicapped animal, a forum to answer to all the questions, and I developed an identity card that would present each animal with a button to share it on the social networks, and another button to make a donation.

After this we voted for our favourite but we could not find an agreement for all of us. After a lot of thinking we decided to created a quiz that would supply informations and raise awareness.

Create a quiz

Our first step to develop the quiz was to list each question, the information link to it and how we could answer to it (checkbox, clickable image, drag...). We got our inspiration from the quiz Slavery Footprint because we wanted to do something funny and not boring.

The First prototype

Then we got our first prototype on Figma and run many user tests. But even if people liked our idea, our little jokes and our illustrations, they were not reading the data. So we decided to rework everything in one day.

Sprint to the end

We worked harder, Paco did even more illustrations and we re-composed the pages thanks to one of our teacher phrase: "On a web-site you have an infinite space don't stay stuck at the top." Because users didn't know where to look at, we had to isolate each element. We finally finished on time and we are really proud of the result.

What did I learn

This workshop learned me a lot about teamwork and how granted to everyone a role can ease the effort. I also learned some skills to take decision through a vote system. Finally, I know now how to use Figma and that when we do something for the second time we can do it faster and better.
