Context

During this week of workshop, we had to do an arcade game without any screen and with alternative controllers. We had to find a simple and attractive gameplay. We quickly chose to do something based on the audio. And while talking, we had the idea of creating an immersive game placing our players inside a submarine.

Rules

Oreille et Torpille (ear & torpedo) is a 2 player audio game. Each player has a wheel to direct their radar, a button to shoot torpedos, 3 LEDs to follow their torpedos' stock, and headphones to follow the game. The goal is to destroy the other submarine. The submarines are static but the radar turn on itself. The player has to listen to the radar, the sound gets higher and higher as the radar gets closer to the other submarine. When the sound is the highest, the player has to shoot with the button. They have to win two rounds to win the game. Each round finishes if one of the two players touches the other or if one doesn't have any torpedo left.

The gameplay

Our game is based on listening and searching. It requires patience, dexterity, and speed.

Material

- 1 arduino card Mega
- 2 arcade buttons
- 2 rotary encoder
- 2 headphones
- 6 red LEDs
- 2 computers

Course of the game

We placed our arcade game below a table, in the dark. The players are next to each other, separated by a curtain. To start the game the players have to push their buttons simultaneously two times. Then they hear a voice telling them that a certain John is not able to do it and they are the ones that need to save the situation, the voice quickly explains how to play and the game starts.
The players don't have any visuals but it's possible to follow the game thanks to the first computer.

Arduino code

The data of the encoders and buttons are captured with Arduino and then send to processing. The LEDs are also managed thanks to Arduino. We had to use a second computer for the player to hear the sounds. That means that both computers are linked by the internet. The main code is in the first computer on processing. It sends the data in real-time to the second computer to say when to play which sound.

Processing code

We had to use a second computer for the player to hear the sounds. That means that both computers are linked by the internet. The main code is in the first computer on processing. It manages the game construction, the scores, and the sounds, and then, it sends the data in real-time to the second computer to say when to play which sound.

The sounds

All of the sounds of the game were done by Paul, he also recorded the voices. Thanks to them the game if really immersive. It gives precious information to the player such as what he has to do if he is losing or winning he also receive the information if the opponent shot in the detection zone.
